How Fragrance Dupes Work

Fragrance dupes (or clones) are affordable alternatives that closely replicate the scent profile of expensive perfumes. They use similar aromatic compounds to achieve a comparable smell at a fraction of the cost. While no dupe is a 100% match — natural ingredients, blending ratios, and aging processes differ — the best dupes are 85–95% similar, especially in the first few hours of wear.

We tested each dupe side-by-side with its original to verify similarity, and rated them on closeness, longevity, and overall value.

Dupe Buying Tips

Buy from authorized retailers. Arabian fragrance houses like Lattafa, Afnan, and Al Haramain are available on Amazon with Prime shipping. Stick to authorized sellers to avoid counterfeits.

Expect 80–90% similarity, not 100%. Even the best dupes differ in dry-down, longevity, or specific facets. That’s normal — you’re still getting the core DNA at a fraction of the cost.

Layer dupes for more complexity. One advantage of cheap dupes is that you can buy several and layer them to create something closer to the original, or even something entirely new.

Some dupes outperform the originals. Afnan 9pm, for example, frequently outlasts Layton in longevity tests. Don’t assume cheap means inferior.

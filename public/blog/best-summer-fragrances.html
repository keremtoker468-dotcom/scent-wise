What Makes a Great Summer Fragrance?

Heat amplifies fragrance projection, so what smells pleasant in winter can become suffocating in July. The best summer scents are built around light, airy notes — citrus, aquatics, green herbs, and sheer musks — that stay refreshing even at 35°C. We prioritized fragrances that project cleanly, don’t turn cloying in humidity, and last at least 4–6 hours on sun-warmed skin.

Summer Fragrance Tips

Apply to pulse points after a shower. Clean, slightly damp skin holds fragrance longer than dry skin. Focus on wrists, neck, and behind the ears.

Switch to lighter concentrations. Eau de Toilette or Eau de Cologne concentrations are ideal for summer. Save your heavy Extrait de Parfum for cooler months.

Keep your bottle cool. Heat degrades fragrance molecules fast. Never leave a bottle in a hot car or on a sunlit shelf.

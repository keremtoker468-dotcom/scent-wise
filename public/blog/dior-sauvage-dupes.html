Best Dior Sauvage Dupes & Alternatives 2026

Dior Sauvage is the best-selling men's fragrance on the planet — but at $100+ per bottle, it is not exactly cheap. Here are 10 alternatives that deliver the same fresh, magnetic, ambroxan-loaded punch for a fraction of the price.

By ScentWise · February 24, 2026 · 9 min read

What Makes Dior Sauvage Special?

Launched in 2015 and perfected across EDT, EDP, Parfum, and Elixir concentrations, Dior Sauvage by Dior is built around a powerful combination of bergamot, Sichuan pepper, ambroxan, and cedar. The result is a clean, slightly spicy, incredibly versatile fragrance that projects confidence without being overwhelming. Perfumer François Demachy created something that transcended trends — it smells modern, masculine, and universally appealing.

At roughly $100–$160 depending on the concentration and size, Sauvage is already more accessible than niche fragrances. But the dupe market has produced some genuinely impressive alternatives that get you 90% of the way there for 20–30% of the price. We analyzed note overlap, longevity reports, and community ratings to find the best ones.

The 10 Best Dior Sauvage Dupes & Alternatives

Each fragrance below shares core DNA with Sauvage — fresh, peppery, ambroxan-rich profiles with woody or aromatic bases. They are ranked by how closely they match the original combined with community satisfaction scores.

The king of Sauvage dupes. While technically closer to Aventus in some batches, the EDP version leans heavily into Sauvage territory with ambroxan, bergamot, and a fresh-spicy profile. Beast-mode projection at a budget price.

Lattafa
$ — ~$15–$25
Lattafa nails the Sauvage DNA here. Bright bergamot opening, peppery heart, and an ambroxan-cedar drydown that is remarkably close to the original EDP. Possibly the best value-for-money Sauvage clone available.

Hawas

Not an exact clone, but occupies the same fresh-spicy-ambroxan space that makes Sauvage so universally appealing. Hawas adds an aquatic twist that makes it an excellent warm-weather alternative. Monster performance.

Cedrat Boisé

$$ — ~$50–$70
A step up in quality and complexity. Cedrat Boisé shares Sauvage's citrus-woody backbone but adds black currant and leather for a slightly more refined, niche-leaning experience. Incredible 10+ hour longevity.

Another Armaf winner that sits between Green Irish Tweed and Sauvage territory. Fresh, clean, and aromatic with a green-woody drydown. A versatile everyday option that punches well above its weight.

Budget vs. Premium: Which Tier Is Right for You?

Under $30 (budget tier): Lattafa Blue de Chance and Maison Alhambra Encode Blue are your best bets. They get you 80–85% of the Sauvage experience for under $25. Longevity is typically 4–6 hours, which is decent but not spectacular.

$30–$70 (mid tier): Armaf Club de Nuit Intense Man and Mancera Cedrat Boisé offer better performance and more complex compositions. Expect 8+ hour longevity and more compliments. This is the sweet spot for most buyers.

$200+ (premium alternatives): Parfums de Marly Percival costs more than Sauvage itself but offers a more refined, niche interpretation of the same DNA. Worth considering if you want something that smells similar but more sophisticated.
